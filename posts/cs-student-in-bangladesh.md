I started at Daffodil International University in January 2025. Three semesters in, here's what I've actually learned — and it's not all about data structures.

## The Academic Reality

The coursework is solid — Data Structures, Algorithms, OOP, Database Systems. But the real education happens outside class. The students who grow the fastest are the ones building side projects at 2 AM, reading research papers on weekends, and joining the right clubs.

## Why I Chose AI

Honestly? I ran GPT-2 locally for the first time and watched it generate coherent paragraphs. Something clicked. The idea that statistical patterns in text could produce apparent *reasoning* felt like the most interesting unsolved puzzle in the world. I've been chasing that feeling ever since.

## The Balance

I play chess to keep my mind sharp and grounded. I write poetry when the technical world feels too abstracted from humanity. Football keeps me moving. These aren't distractions — they're the things that make me a better thinker.

If you're a fellow student feeling overwhelmed: it's okay. Build one thing. Read one paper. The compound interest of small consistent actions is the whole game.
